SensorGnome User Guide

Chapter 4 Transferring Data and Files

In order to download detection data, update the software of your SensorGnome, or modify configuration files you must use one of the following methods to transfers files to and from the SensorGnome.

4.1 Option 1: FTP Connection

An FTP Connection is the preferred method of transferring files in most cases. It allows data to be transferred without powering down the SG, and it also ensures the Web Interface can also be checked at the same time, which is an important step when working with your SG. In these examples, we use the free and open-source FileZilla program, though the process would be similar for other FTP clients. The instructions are the same for both Raspberry Pi and BeagleBone SG’s, though the credentials needed to connect are different.

  1. Connect to the SensorGnome using the process described above

  2. Open FileZilla and enter the remote host address and login credentials in the top left bar. The host address differs depending on whether you are a RPi or BB SG. In each case you will log in as user: root and password: root.

Figure 4.1: Enter the host address and credentials in the top left. These are the credentials for a Raspberry Pi SensorGnome.

Once connected, the two left hand panels will represent the folder structure on your own local computer, and the right hand panel will represent the folder structure on the SensorGnome. If you are downloaded detection files, you will need to navigate to the SGdata folder. If you are modifying the deployment.txt file, applying a software patch, or configuring internet access over WiFi, you will need to navigate to the uboot folder.

Tip: You can save the connection configuration as well as create bookmarks for specific paths in FileZilla. This will save a lot of time when in the field.

4.2 Option 2: Removing the MicroSD Card

Removing the MicroSD card is fairly straightforward, but comes with two strong warnings.

  • Always ensure that the Raspberry Pi or BeagleBone computer is completely powered down when removing or replacing the card. Failure to do say may result in power loss. Note that the BeagleBone has two potential sources of power: the 5V barrel jack (the main source) as well as the Mini USB which can also power the BB.

  • For Raspberry Pi SensorGnomes, the software that powers the SG is on the MicroSD card, and not installed on internal memory. If you remove the card, or if you replace the card with another that does not have the software installed on it, the SG will not run properly when you turn it back on.

Once the SG is powered down, remove the MicroSD card.

  • You may need tweezers to remove the card from the Raspberry Pi as it is deeply recessed into the slot.

  • You may also see a label to the effect of “Do not remove this card.” That is due to the warnings given above and can be disregarded.

  • You may see a message on your computer saying that the card needs to be formatted, especially with cards that had been used in BeagleBones. In most cases you can ignore this.

4.2.1 Raspberry Pi

To copy detection data from the MicroSD card, navigate on your computer to the SGdata folder and download the desired folders.

Because the software for a RPi SG runs off the MicroSD card, you can also make direct changes to configuration, such as modifying the deployment.txt and network.txt files, as well as to apply a software patch.

4.2.2 BeagleBone

Copying detection data is the only task that can be achieved by removing the MicroSD card.

4.3 Option 3: Shared network drive

This only works with a BeagleBone based SensorGnome. It is not the recommended method of transferring files but can be occasionally be useful as an alternate method, especially for BeagleBones that cannot be properly accessed via FTP.

In your file explorer on your computer, visit \\192.168.7.2\root.
